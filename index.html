Boxuan is currently working as an Assistant Professor in the Faculty of Arts and Science at Kyushu University. He is also an adjunct member of the School of Interdisciplinary Science and Innovation at Kyushu University. He received the B.S. and M.S. degrees from Southwest Jiaotong University, China, in 2014 and 2017, respectively, and the Ph.D. degree from Kyushu University, Fukuoka, Japan, in 2021. His main research interests include Artificial Intelligence in Education, Educational Data Mining, Learning Analytics, Human-Computer Interaction, and Recommender Systems.   1. Personalized Course Recommendation

    2. We conduct research on personalized course recommendation systems. This involved investigating students' course-choice motivations, developing novel course-recommendation algorithms, and designing explainable, interactive recommendation interfaces to better support students' diverse needs and preferences.

  1. E-book Reading Behavior Analysis

    2. We investigate students' reading processes in e-book environments, including general reading approaches and specific reading behaviors, to understand how learners engage with e-books and how these processes relate to their metacognition and performance. We also design intelligent support for e-book learning systems, including personalized navigation recommendations and adaptive dashboards.

  1. Language Learning Support

    2. We conduct research on personalized language learning support, including deep-learning-based cognitive-diagnosis models to estimate learners' language proficiency, knowledge-tracing models to predict memory retention, and spaced-repetition scheduling methods to support long-term retention. These models have been deployed in a widely used language-learning app in Japan and have been used by millions of students.

  1. LLM-based Programming Learning Assistant

    2. We examine how students interact with AI and how AI influences student engagement, problem-solving processes, and learning experiences in computing education. We also explore effective ways to integrate AI into computing education by deriving design implications for AI-based programming learning assistants and by developing novel systems that provide learning-oriented scaffolding while preserving students' reasoning and agency.
